March 2016  |  Napier, Royal Opera House

BRIEF

Encourage culturally engaged young people aged 20-30 to experience opera for the first time.

INSIGHT

Many people within the target audience would not visit an opera because it was too long and boring.

CONCEPT

An opera scene or storyline was summarised in 140 characters, resembling how the younger generation view information on Twitter today. Hashtags would be used to add humour and extend the sense of modernity.

OUTCOME

Bursts of typographic animation would be projected on a city's surface or building during the evening to allow the projections to be visible. As a result, the campaign would be displayed when the target audience are more likely to be out, socialising and open-minded to various happenings.

SCALED MODEL

The projections were designed to be adaptable to different locations. However, throughout the design process we worked with a model of Buchanan Galleries in Glasgow. We felt this area was a good example of a public crossroads where the target audience would be, and were able to pause to watch the projection advertisement.

This project was a joint effort between myself and a fellow university student, Mary-Jo Daly. We were both over-the-moon when YCN commended the work and included it in their 2016 annual.
